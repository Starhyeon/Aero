Blackwater Treatment

Blackwater from toilets (75L daily, 25% of total wastewater) receives conventional septic treatment through a robust 1,500L two-chamber concrete tank. The first chamber provides primary settlement where solids separate and begin anaerobic digestion‚Äîbeneficial bacteria breaking down organic matter without oxygen, generating biogas (mostly methane) that vents safely. The second chamber provides additional settling and clarification. Clarified effluent then flows to a 200 sq ft subsurface leach field where soil bacteria complete treatment through aerobic decomposition. The system requires pumping every 3-5 years to remove accumulated solids. Alternative option: composting toilets eliminate blackwater entirely while producing valuable compost, saving 27,375L water annually and eliminating septic maintenance‚Äîbut requiring active management. Both approaches successfully manage human waste without environmental contamination.
